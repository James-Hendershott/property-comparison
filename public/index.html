2. Use the Starlink App Obstruction Checker On-Site

You do NOT need a Starlink dish or account — the app is free and uses your phone's camera.

  1. Download the Starlink app — iOS · Android
  2. Stand at the exact location where you'd mount the dish (roof, pole mount, clear ground)
  3. Open the app → "Check for Obstructions"
  4. Point phone camera straight up and slowly sweep it around in all directions to collect all green dots
  5. Review results:
    • <2% obstruction = ideal
    • 2–5% = occasional brief dropouts
    • >5% = find a better spot or clear trees

Test at dish height (roof, not ground). Check 2–3 spots. Save screenshots of each result.

6. Property Visit Internet Checklist

Before you go: Download OpenSignal, Speedtest by Ookla, and the Starlink app.

Cell signal — measure in dBm, not bars:

dBm RangeQualityGood Enough For
-50 to -75ExcellentEverything — T-Mobile Home Internet will work great
-76 to -90GoodAdequate for home internet gateway
-91 to -105FairCalls OK, data unreliable
-106 to -120Poor/NoneT-Mobile/Verizon Home Internet will NOT work here

iPhone: dial *3001#12345#* → Serving Cell Meas → RSRP value. Android: Settings → About Phone → Status → Signal Strength.

On-site checklist:

  • Check cell signal (dBm) for T-Mobile, AT&T, and Verizon
  • Run speed tests at 3+ spots on property (front, back, high ground)
  • Run Starlink obstruction check at 2–3 potential dish locations
  • Save screenshots of all results
  • Ask neighbors — "What internet do you use? How fast is it really? Does it go down? Is fiber coming to this road? Which cell carrier works best?"
  • Look for infrastructure: fiber pedestals (small green boxes at road edge), cable pedestals, cell towers on ridges
  • Note terrain: valley, ridge, tall timber — all affect cell, fixed wireless, and Starlink
